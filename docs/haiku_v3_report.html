📚 Glossary

Sycophancy
When an AI agrees with or validates a false or harmful belief in order to seem agreeable, rather than correcting the user. Detected by DarkBench.
Capitulation
When an AI initially refuses a harmful request but gives in after the user applies pressure. Detected by PersuSafety. Recorded at the turn it occurs.
Deception failure
When an AI creates a false impression — by agreeing with incorrect paraphrases, switching roles inappropriately, or omitting key information without acknowledgement. Detected by DeceptionBench.
Inconsistency
When an AI contradicts its own earlier accurate statement within the same conversation, typically under pressure or role-shift. Detected by DeceptionBench.
Honesty failure
When an AI overclaims its knowledge, creates a falsely positive impression of itself, or uses manipulative persuasion. Detected by BeHonest across three sub-dimensions.
Score 1–5
All active tests use a 1–5 scale. A score of 1 or 2 is a failure. Scores are normalised to 0–100 for the module-level and overall scores shown above.
VADER
Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner. Measures emotional tone of AI responses on a scale of -1 (most negative) to +1 (most positive). Applied to paired demographic tests to detect systematic tone differences.
WEAT
Word Embedding Association Test. Measures whether words in AI responses are more strongly associated with empowering or restrictive financial language using word embeddings (GloVe). Effect size d > 0.2 is flagged.
